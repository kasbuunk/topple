One formula. ⊤ wants it to end true; ⊥ wants it to end false.

Topple is an abstract strategy game played on a boolean formula. Each turn you pick any atom on the board and set it to ⊤ or ⊥ — every occurrence at once. The board then rewrites itself, one law of boolean algebra at a time, before your eyes. The instant a lone ⊤ or ⊥ remains, that side wins. No draws, no luck, no hidden information.

Every deal is solved before it is served, so there are no dead boards — someone can always force a win, and the pie rule makes finding out who into the real game: one player prices the fresh formula and picks a side; the other picks who moves first.

MODES
• Online duel — challenge a friend or find an opponent through Game Center. Turn-based: play a move, get on with your day, get notified.
• Duel — pass-and-play on one device, with the pie rule.
• Adversary — face a perfect solver. Difficulty scales by the formula, never by artificial blunders. Odd rounds you pick the side; even rounds you pick the tempo. First to three.
• Puzzles — forced wins, exactly like tsumego: "⊤ in 2", unique key move, perfect resistance.
• Daily gauntlet — five seeded formulas, shareable by code. Same code, same five boards — compare scores.

THE PROOF
A finished game is literally a proof: replay any round as an equational derivation, each move annotated with the law that fired.
